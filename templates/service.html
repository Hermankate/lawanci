Empowered Women Influence - South Sudan Project

Overview

This overview highlights the progress made in the month of July and August 2024 by the 2 Women Advocacy Groups while implementing the Empowered Women Influence project in two separate regions (Greater Gogrial and Greater Tonj) of Warrap state after receiving their seed grand from LAWANCI in June to create Sexual/Gender-Based Violence (SGBV) Awareness and Lobby at the sub national (Twic, Gogrial East, Gogrial West, Tonj South, Tonj North, and Tonj East Counties, and all Payams and Bomas) and address the pervasive issue of SGBV in the community. The activities aimed to raise awareness about SGBV, provide support to survivors, Influence and advocate for policy making process/ changes to prevent SGBV and gender equality.

Activities

What was done

Community Outreach

Conducted community meetings, workshops, and training sessions to raise awareness about SGBV.

Capacity Building

The WAGs trained community leaders Chiefs from the Payams and Bomas, healthcare providers on handling S/GBV cases, and law enforcement officials(police) on SGBV response and prevention.

Support Services

The WAGs trained Women in the Payams and Bomas who monitors cases of SGBV in their community and report, provides advices to parents and counselling, legal aid referral and follow ups, and medical support to victims.

Advocacy Efforts

Women Advocacy Groups met with the Local Chiefs, women leaders in the community, and aim to meet policymakers of Warrap state to submit petitions, and organize protests to push for policy changes and equitable service provision as well as implementation of the 35% of women representation in the leadership at the state level.

Achievements

What was achieved

Women Advocacy Groups of Warrap state managed to reached over 2,000 community members through awareness campaigns in a workshops, trainings, and radio talk shows..

The 2 Women Advocacy Groups in Warrap state trained over 250 community leaders, selected women in the community, and school girls on SGBV response.

These Women Advocacy Group managed to identify 3 cases of rape, 1 early marriage, 2 force marriages in the community of Tonj East and South, Gogrial east and provided support services to survivors within July and August (taken the 2 raped young girls for medical examination, counselling to their parents and victims, and the referred cases to the authority for further follow up).

On 25th July to 1st August, Women Advocacy Group of greater Gogrial successfully completed training on effective advocacy and lobby provided by SSUDEMOP to start on lobby for the construction of kuajok hospital in Gogrial West, East and twic county. Tonj hospital in Tonj North, East, and South County. And the review of traditional Law that should include Women Views and answer question of women rights and protecting survivors of SGBV in the society.

Challenges

what challenges we are facing

  • Limited resources and funding. Due to skyrocketing inflation, the price of goods and services has risen, limiting most planned programs. Costs for venue hire, tents, transportation, feeding, and mobilization airtime have increased.
  • Resistance from some community members and leaders, especially related to gender stereotypes. Some women defend their daughters' early marriages as a business, marrying them off young for financial gain. Men who commit crimes often go unpunished in villages.
  • Limited access to some community members due to bad roads, conflicting communities, and the underdeveloped Warrap state.
  • Stigmatization prevents survivors from seeking help, reporting incidents, and accessing essential services.
  • Inter-communal conflict. In July, a revenge killing between Ngabagok Payam and Lwanyjang/Romich Boma communities in Tonj East County resulted in the loss of 8 lives, primarily targeting farmers.
  • Continuous cattle raiding. In late August, youth from Tonj Payam raided cattle from Manyangok Payam in Tonj South County, raising fear among community members.

Recommendations

  • Increase funding and resources for SGBV initiatives. More funding will enable a wider spread of SGBV education to the public. “Through LAWANCI we request PAX to support us with a Car to enable us to reach the whole areas of our concern in Warrap state”.
  • Strengthen partnerships with policymakers and service providers. Partnerships at the national level can create opportunities for Women Groups to meet with legislators and have their voices heard.
