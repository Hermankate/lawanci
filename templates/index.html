Legacy for African Women and Children Initiative (LAWANCI) is a national Non-Governmental Organization incorporated on the 13th February 2018 in the Republic of South Sudan with the main aim of empowering, encouraging and promoting the safety of women and children through education, economic, health and psychological support services across the continent.
The mission for Legacy for African Women and Children Initiative (LAWANCI) is to empower, encourage and promote the safety of children and women through education, economic, health and psychological support services across the continent.
The vision Legacy for African Women and Children Initiative (LAWANCI) is to create an African society that recognizes the legacy of women and children.

Our Objectives

What We Aim to Achieve

Empowerment

To empower women and children through education, skill development, and economic opportunities.

Health & Safety

To promote health and ensure the safety of women and children by providing necessary health services and awareness programs.

Advocacy

To advocate for the rights of women and children and work towards eliminating discrimination and violence.

Peaceful Co-existence

Encourage peaceful co-existence among communities and citizens by promoting peace programs designed to strengthen and enhance women and children's participation in peace processes.

Civic & Cultural Programs

Support civic, social, and cultural programs designed to increase the role of women and children in enhancing peace in the country.

Skill Development

Create opportunities for women and children to gain skills and knowledge which they can use to sustain their livelihoods.

Research and Policy

In partnership with relevant institutions, use research to create positive societal changes that would reinforce and inform policies that will promote and safeguard the interests of women and children across all sectors.
